Am I doing enough?

So you do what any caring parent would do: you Google "how to teach my toddler at home."

50 million results. Flashcard sets. Montessori kits for $200. Pinterest boards with craft projects that require a degree in fine arts. YouTube channels with creepy animated characters your kid is somehow already addicted to.

Sound familiar?

The Uncomfortable Truth About Your Child's Most Important Years

Here's something most parents don't hear until it's too late:

90%
of a child's brain development happens
before age 5

That's not a motivational poster quote. That's neuroscience. The connections your child's brain builds in these first few years literally shape their capacity to learn, regulate emotions, and solve problems — for the rest of their life.

But here's the problem: the systems meant to support this development are broken.

  • Preschools are overwhelmed. One teacher for every 12-15 kids means almost zero 1-on-1 attention during the most critical learning window.
  • Screen time has exploded. The average toddler now gets 2-3 hours of screen time per day — and climbing.
  • Most "educational" toys are marketing gimmicks. $40 plastic gadgets that get ignored after 5 minutes.
  • Parents are drowning in options. Too many choices, too little clarity about what actually works.

"I knew what my kids needed to learn. I just couldn't find a simple, affordable way to teach it at home."

That quote comes from thousands of moms. But it also comes from one in particular — a PreK teacher from Texas who decided to fix the problem herself.

She Spent Years Teaching Other People's Kids. Then She Had Her Own.

Becca didn't set out to build a company. She set out to help her students.

As a PreK teacher in Texas, she saw the gap every single day: kids who came from homes with learning tools on the walls — alphabet charts, number lines, color wheels — showed up to school already ahead. Not because they were smarter. Because their environment had done the work.

The kids without those tools? They spent the first three months of school catching up on things they could have absorbed at home, almost by accident, just by walking past a poster every day.

Becca started Hadley Designs over 10 years ago — first as a small Etsy shop, now trusted by over 2 million families.

Then Becca became a mom. Four times over. And suddenly, she wasn't just observing the gap — she was living it.

"I was a literal preschool teacher, and I still felt overwhelmed trying to teach my own kids at home," she says. "That's when I realized: if it's hard for me, it must feel impossible for most parents."

So she started designing what she wished had existed: simple, beautiful, visual learning tools based on the actual PreK curriculum she taught every day. She tested them on her own kids first. Then she posted a few on Etsy.

That was over 10 years ago.

What happened next surprised everyone — including Becca.
